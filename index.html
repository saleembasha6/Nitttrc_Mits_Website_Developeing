about us

The majority of individuals living in rural areas and slums require assistance in adopting appropriate technology to improve their productivity and living standards. In response to this need, the National Institute of Technical Teachers Training and Research (NITTTR) established the Centre for Rural Development in 1979. The center aimed to create an extensive network that could facilitate the adoption of suitable technologies among rural communities and slum dwellers.

Recognizing the importance of rural entrepreneurship in enhancing the quality of life in rural areas, the center underwent a transformation and was renamed the "Centre for Rural and Entrepreneurship Development" (CRED).
